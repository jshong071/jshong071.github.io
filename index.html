Research/Publications

SAND: Smooth Imputation of Sparse and Noisy Functional Data with Transformer Networks

Authors: Ju-Sheng Hong, Junwen Yao, Jonas Mueller, Jane-Ling Wang

Conference: The Thirty-eighth Annual Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS 2024)

Summary: SAND enhances transformer models for functional data imputation by introducing a Self-Attention on Derivatives (SAND) module. This ensures smooth imputations without requiring assumptions about the data distribution. Theoretical guarantees and empirical results demonstrate SAND's superior performance compared to traditional and existing neural methods.

Model-based Hypothesis Tests for the Causal Mediation of Semi-Competing Risks

Authors: Yun-Lin Ho, Ju-Sheng Hong, Yen-Tsung Huang

Journal: Lifetime Data Analysis, Vol. 30, Issue 1, Pages 119-142 (Springer US, January 2024)

Summary: This paper addresses the causal mediation of semi-competing risks, where an intermediate event may be censored by a primary event but not vice versa. It introduces model-based hypothesis tests, including an intersection-union test (IUT) and a weighted log-rank test (WLR), under the counterfactual outcome framework. These methods decompose the effect of an exposure into direct and indirect effects, ensuring proper adjustment for confounders. The tests demonstrated significant results in analyzing the mediation effects of hepatitis B/C on liver cancer risk through liver cirrhosis incidence.
